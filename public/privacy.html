Lanecraft - Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 15, 2026

Lanecraft ("the Game") is a free game developed by Krool ("we", "us"). This policy explains what data the Game collects and how it is used.

Data We Collect

The Game uses Firebase (by Google) for optional online multiplayer features. When you use these features, the following data may be collected:

  • Anonymous identifier — Firebase Authentication creates a random anonymous ID to identify your device. No email, name, or login is required.
  • Multiplayer game data — If you play online multiplayer, game commands and synchronization data are temporarily stored in Firebase Realtime Database for the duration of the match.

Data We Do Not Collect

  • We do not collect your real name, email address, phone number, or any personal information.
  • We do not collect location data.
  • We do not use advertising SDKs or ad tracking.
  • We do not sell or share data with third parties (beyond Firebase infrastructure).

Local Storage

The Game saves your progress, settings, player profile, and preferences locally on your device using browser local storage. This data never leaves your device.

Third-Party Services

Firebase (Google) processes the anonymous identifier and multiplayer data described above. Google's privacy policy applies to their processing: https://policies.google.com/privacy

Children's Privacy

The Game does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. Since we only use anonymous identifiers and locally stored player-chosen display names, no personal information is collected from any user.

Data Deletion

To delete your data, clear the app's local storage (uninstall and reinstall). Multiplayer game data is automatically deleted from Firebase after each match ends.

Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. The effective date at the top will be updated accordingly.

Contact

For questions about this privacy policy, open an issue at: github.com/Krool/SpawnWars/issues
